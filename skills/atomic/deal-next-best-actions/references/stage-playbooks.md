# Stage-by-Stage Playbooks

Complete playbooks for each deal stage with required actions, exit criteria, risk indicators, time limits, emergency interventions for stalled deals, and a worked example showing optimal actions through a full deal journey. Use this reference to determine what "good" looks like at each stage and when a deal is off-track.

## Playbook Overview

Each stage playbook follows the same structure:
- **Objective**: What you are trying to accomplish at this stage
- **Top 5 Actions**: Non-negotiable actions with impact ratings
- **Exit Criteria**: Verifiable conditions before advancing
- **Risk Indicators**: Signals the deal is going sideways
- **Time Limits**: Maximum duration before intervention
- **Common Mistakes**: What reps do wrong at this stage
- **Minimum Viable Action**: The single most important thing if short on time

### Stage Duration Benchmarks

| Stage | SMB Target | Mid-Market Target | Enterprise Target | Red Flag Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery | 3-5 days | 5-10 days | 1-3 weeks | 2x target |
| Qualification | 3-5 days | 5-10 days | 1-2 weeks | 2x target |
| Demo / Evaluation | 5-10 days | 10-21 days | 3-6 weeks | 2x target |
| Proposal | 3-5 days | 5-10 days | 1-3 weeks | 2x target |
| Negotiation | 3-7 days | 7-14 days | 2-6 weeks | 2x target |
| Closing | 1-3 days | 3-7 days | 1-3 weeks | 2x target |

Lost deals average 1.6x longer per stage than won deals (InsightSquared, Salesforce State of Sales 2023).

## Stage 1: Discovery

### Objective
Determine whether this buyer has a real problem that your solution can solve, with the urgency and budget to act on it. Discovery is qualification -- if the answers do not support a real opportunity, stop here.

### Top 5 Actions

| # | Action | Impact | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Conduct discovery call with open-ended pain questions | 5 | 30-45 min | Rep |
| 2 | Quantify the pain in dollars or hours | 5 | 15-30 min | Rep + Buyer |
| 3 | Identify at least 2 stakeholders by name | 4 | During call | Rep |
| 4 | Document the buying process | 4 | 5-10 min | Rep |
| 5 | Send meeting recap with insight within 24 hours | 3 | 15-20 min | Rep |

### Exit Criteria

All must be true before advancing:
- [ ] Buyer articulated a specific pain in their own words
- [ ] Pain has a financial dimension ($X/month, Y hours lost, Z revenue at risk)
- [ ] At least one other stakeholder identified by name
- [ ] Buyer confirmed this is a priority (not "nice to have, maybe next year")
- [ ] Next step scheduled with a date and time

### Risk Indicators

| Signal | Severity | What It Means | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Just exploring" | Medium | No urgency | Ask: "What would make this a priority in the next 90 days?" |
| Only one contact | High | Single-threaded from day 1 | Ask for stakeholder introduction before next call |
| No financial quantification | Medium | Pain may not be funded | Share ROI data from similar companies |
| Avoids discussing budget | Medium | Budget may not exist | Ask: "Has budget been allocated, or would you need to request it?" |
| No next meeting after discovery | High | Buyer not committed | Send recap with proposed next step and date |

### Time Limit
- SMB: 5 business days | Mid-Market: 10 days | Enterprise: 15 days

### Common Mistakes
1. **Pitching too early**: Talking about your product before understanding their problem. Discovery is about THEM, not you.
2. **Accepting vague pain**: "We want to improve efficiency" is not a pain statement. Push for specifics: which process, how much time, what cost.
3. **Not multi-threading**: Leaving the first call with only one contact guarantees a fragile deal.
4. **Skipping the recap**: Without a written record of what was discussed, the buyer forgets 80% within a week.

### Minimum Viable Action
Send the meeting recap with insight within 24 hours. 15 minutes, keeps the deal moving.

## Stage 2: Qualification

### Objective
Confirm the opportunity meets qualification criteria (BANT, MEDDIC, or org framework). Decide: invest or disqualify.

### Top 5 Actions

| # | Action | Impact | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Validate Budget: confirmed or path to approval | 5 | During call | Rep |
| 2 | Validate Authority: decision maker(s) by name | 5 | During call | Rep |
| 3 | Validate Need: pain is real, funded, urgent | 5 | During call | Rep |
| 4 | Validate Timeline: compelling event or deadline | 4 | During call | Rep |
| 5 | Identify the champion (internal advocate) | 4 | During call | Rep |

### MEDDIC Quick Reference

| Letter | Element | Key Question |
|---|---|---|
| M | Metrics | "How will you measure success?" |
| E | Economic Buyer | "Who controls the budget for this?" |
| D | Decision Criteria | "What are the top 3 things that matter?" |
| D | Decision Process | "Walk me through yes to signed contract." |
| I | Identify Pain | "What happens if you do nothing for 6 months?" |
| C | Champion | "Who on your team is most excited about solving this?" |

### Exit Criteria

- [ ] Budget exists or clear path to approval documented
- [ ] Decision maker identified by name
- [ ] Need is real: buyer actively looking, not just researching
- [ ] Compelling event drives timeline (regulation, budget cycle, competitor, mandate)
- [ ] Deal fits ICP (industry, size, use case)
- [ ] Champion identified who will advocate internally

### Risk Indicators

| Signal | Severity | What It Means | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| "No budget yet" | High | Must build business case | Shift to business case; timeline extends |
| Cannot name decision maker | Critical | Will stall at proposal | Ask directly: "Who signs the contract?" |
| No compelling event | High | Deal will drag | Help find one: budget cycle, regulation, competitive threat |
| Low-level champion (IC) | Medium | May lack influence | Ask them to introduce their manager |
| No evaluation criteria | Medium | Decision will be slow | Help create criteria (favoring your strengths) |

### Time Limit
- SMB: 5 days | Mid-Market: 10 days | Enterprise: 15 days

### Common Mistakes
1. **Skipping qualification entirely**: Moving to demo because the buyer is excited, without confirming they can actually buy.
2. **Accepting "my boss will decide"**: You need a name, a role, and a path to reach them.
3. **Not finding the compelling event**: Without urgency, deals drift into "next quarter" forever.

### Minimum Viable Action
Confirm the economic buyer by name. Everything else flows from knowing who controls the budget.

## Stage 3: Demo / Evaluation

### Objective
Prove your solution solves their specific problem better than alternatives. Build conviction with multiple stakeholders, not just one person.

### Top 5 Actions

| # | Action | Impact | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Deliver customized demo using buyer's use case | 5 | 1-2h prep + 45 min | Rep + SE |
| 2 | Engage at least 3 stakeholders directly | 5 | Ongoing | Rep |
| 3 | Arrange reference call with peer customer | 5 | 30 min to arrange | Rep |
| 4 | Build ROI model with buyer's actual numbers | 5 | 1-2 hours | Rep |
| 5 | Engage economic buyer directly | 4 | 30 min meeting | Rep + Exec |

### Exit Criteria

- [ ] Demo delivered using buyer's specific scenarios
- [ ] 3+ stakeholders have engaged with your team
- [ ] Technical fit confirmed (integration, security, performance)
- [ ] Reference call completed or case study reviewed
- [ ] ROI model built with buyer-validated numbers
- [ ] Economic buyer aware and supportive
- [ ] Evaluation criteria documented, solution scores favorably
- [ ] Buyer agrees to move to proposal

### Risk Indicators

| Signal | Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Demo keeps being postponed | Critical | Ask: "Is this still a priority?" |
| Only champion attends demo | High | Schedule separate session for technical team |
| New evaluation criteria appear late | High | Ask: "What prompted these new criteria?" (competitor influence?) |
| "We need to see more" (no specifics) | Medium | Ask: "Specifically, what would you need to see?" |
| Technical blockers raised | Medium-High | Schedule deep-dive with your SE |

### Time Limit
- SMB: 10 days | Mid-Market: 21 days | Enterprise: 6 weeks

### Common Mistakes
1. **Generic demo**: Showing features instead of solving their problem. Customized demos close at 2.25x the rate.
2. **Ignoring the competition**: If you do not know who else they are evaluating, you cannot differentiate.
3. **No ROI model**: The economic buyer needs numbers, not features.
4. **Waiting too long for reference call**: Reference calls after proposal lose half their impact.

### Minimum Viable Action
Schedule the demo with at least 2 stakeholders in the room.

## Stage 4: Proposal

### Objective
Deliver a proposal the buyer can say "yes" to without additional back-and-forth. The proposal stage should be SHORT -- selling happened in evaluation.

### Top 5 Actions

| # | Action | Impact | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Send proposal within 48h of evaluation completion | 5 | 1-2 hours | Rep |
| 2 | Include 3 pricing tiers with clear differentiation | 4 | In proposal | Rep |
| 3 | Pre-wire negotiation with champion | 5 | 15 min call | Rep |
| 4 | Follow up on proposal within 48 hours | 4 | 10 min | Rep |
| 5 | Identify procurement requirements | 4 | 10 min question | Rep |

### Exit Criteria

- [ ] Proposal delivered and confirmed received
- [ ] Buyer has reviewed the proposal
- [ ] Pricing tier selected or counterproposal received
- [ ] No new stakeholders appearing
- [ ] Procurement requirements documented
- [ ] Next step is negotiation or signature

### Risk Indicators

| Signal | Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Proposal not opened (3+ days) | High | Call: "Did you get a chance to review?" |
| New stakeholders at proposal | Critical | Assess: champion or blocker? |
| 30%+ discount request | High | Ask: "Help me understand the budget constraints" |
| "Need to run by my boss" | High | Economic buyer missed earlier; offer exec meeting |
| Radio silence after proposal | Critical | Follow up 48h, escalate to different contact at 5 days |

### Time Limit
- SMB: 5 days | Mid-Market: 10 days | Enterprise: 15 days

### Common Mistakes
1. **Waiting to perfect the proposal**: A good proposal today beats a perfect proposal next week.
2. **Not pre-wiring**: Surprises in negotiation mean you missed something.
3. **Single pricing option**: One price = take it or leave it. Three tiers = buyer chooses (and usually picks the middle).

### Minimum Viable Action
Send the proposal. Now.

## Stage 5: Negotiation

### Objective
Remove every obstacle between "yes" and signature. The buyer already wants to buy. This is about process, not persuasion.

### Top 5 Actions

| # | Action | Impact | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Resolve commercial terms (pricing, SLA, contract) | 5 | 30-60 min | Rep |
| 2 | Support legal review (MSA, DPA, security) | 4 | 1-4 weeks (ent.) | Rep + Legal |
| 3 | Complete procurement requirements (PO, vendor reg.) | 4 | 1-3 weeks | Buyer + Rep |
| 4 | Have concessions ready (do not give away unprompted) | 4 | 15 min prep | Rep |
| 5 | Maintain champion engagement throughout | 3 | Ongoing | Rep |

**Concession hierarchy** (least to most costly):
1. Extended payment terms (Net-60 vs. Net-30)
2. Additional training or onboarding hours
3. Longer pilot or trial period
4. Multi-year discount (locks revenue)
5. Added features or users (low marginal cost for SaaS)
6. Price reduction (last resort -- tie to commitment)

### Exit Criteria

- [ ] All commercial terms agreed
- [ ] Legal review complete
- [ ] Security review complete
- [ ] Procurement process done (vendor registered, PO issued)
- [ ] Final approver identified and aligned
- [ ] Contract ready for signature

### Risk Indicators

| Signal | Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Legal scope expanding | High | Direct contact between legal teams |
| Champion goes quiet | Critical | Call: "Is everything OK on your end?" |
| New procurement requirements | Medium | Get the full list upfront |
| "Let me think about it" | High | "What specifically are you weighing?" |
| Competitor mentioned | Critical | "Still evaluating, or about best terms?" |

### Time Limit
- SMB: 7 days | Mid-Market: 14 days | Enterprise: 6 weeks

### Common Mistakes
1. **Giving concessions without getting something back**: Every concession should be traded, not gifted.
2. **Losing touch with the champion**: During legal/procurement, your champion is your insider. Keep them informed.
3. **Not mapping the full procurement process upfront**: Discovering requirements one at a time extends the cycle.

### Minimum Viable Action
Ask: "What is the one thing standing between us and getting this done?" Then resolve it.

## Stage 6: Closing

### Objective
Get the contract signed and set up for successful implementation. Speed is everything -- every day is a day something can go wrong.

### Top 5 Actions

| # | Action | Impact | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Send e-sign contract (pre-filled, mobile-friendly) | 5 | 10 min | Rep |
| 2 | Confirm authorized signatory is available | 4 | 5 min | Rep |
| 3 | Follow up within 4 hours if not signed same day | 5 | 5 min | Rep |
| 4 | Share implementation plan preview | 4 | 30 min | Rep + CS |
| 5 | Schedule kickoff meeting before contract signed | 3 | 10 min | Rep |

### Exit Criteria

- [ ] Contract signed by authorized signatories
- [ ] Payment received or PO confirmed
- [ ] Implementation kickoff date set
- [ ] Success metrics agreed for first 90 days
- [ ] Customer success team introduced

### Risk Indicators

| Signal | Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Contract not opened | Critical | Call champion, confirm signatory has it |
| "One more meeting" | High | "What specifically do we need to cover?" |
| Close date pushed 2nd time | Critical | "Is there something blocking this?" |
| Champion suddenly unavailable | Critical | Contact another stakeholder immediately |

### Time Limit
- SMB: 3 days | Mid-Market: 7 days | Enterprise: 15 days

### Common Mistakes
1. **PDF attachment instead of e-sign**: Adds days of friction.
2. **Not following up same day**: Urgency dies overnight.
3. **No implementation preview**: Buyer fears the unknown after signing.

### Minimum Viable Action
Send the e-sign link. If already sent, follow up by phone within 4 hours.

## Stage 7: Post-Sale Handoff

### Objective
Ensure the customer starts successfully and the relationship transitions smoothly from sales to customer success.

### Top 5 Actions

| # | Action | Impact | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Internal handoff meeting (sales to CS) | 4 | 30 min | Rep + CS |
| 2 | Customer kickoff meeting | 5 | 45-60 min | CS + Rep |
| 3 | Share 90-day success plan with customer | 4 | 30 min to prep | CS |
| 4 | Introduce all customer-facing team members | 3 | 10 min | Rep |
| 5 | Schedule 30-day check-in | 3 | 5 min | CS |

### Common Mistakes
1. **Ghosting after the signature**: The customer just trusted you with their money. Disappearing destroys the relationship.
2. **Not briefing CS**: Customer success should not have to re-discover what sales already learned.
3. **No success metrics**: Without agreed metrics, the customer cannot tell if the purchase was worth it.

## Stage Transition Checklist

| Transition | Must Be True |
|---|---|
| Discovery -> Qualification | Pain quantified, 1+ stakeholder identified, next step scheduled |
| Qualification -> Evaluation | BANT confirmed, champion identified, evaluation criteria agreed |
| Evaluation -> Proposal | Demo completed, 3+ stakeholders, EB aware, technical fit confirmed |
| Proposal -> Negotiation | Proposal reviewed, tier selected, procurement requirements known |
| Negotiation -> Closing | All terms agreed, legal complete, approver aligned |
| Closing -> Won | Contract signed, payment confirmed, kickoff scheduled |
| Won -> Post-Sale | Internal handoff done, kickoff scheduled, 90-day plan shared |

**The premature advancement trap**: Moving a deal forward before exit criteria are met inflates the pipeline and destroys forecast accuracy. A deal in "Proposal" without EB engagement is really still in "Evaluation." Be honest about stage placement.

## Stage-Specific Metrics

### What to Track at Each Stage

| Stage | Key Metrics | What They Tell You |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | Days to first response, discovery call completion rate, pain quantification rate | Whether leads are engaged and whether discovery is thorough |
| Qualification | BANT completion rate, champion identification rate, disqualification rate | Quality of pipeline entering evaluation (healthy pipelines disqualify 30-40%) |
| Evaluation | Demo-to-proposal conversion, stakeholders engaged per deal, time in evaluation | Whether demos are effective and whether multi-threading is happening |
| Proposal | Proposal send time (hours after verbal yes), proposal open rate, tier selection | Speed and engagement with the commercial offer |
| Negotiation | Discount rate, concession count, legal review duration | Commercial health and process efficiency |
| Closing | Days to signature, signature-to-kickoff time | Final velocity and handoff quality |

### Pipeline Health Indicators

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|---|---|---|---|
| Average days in any stage | At or below target | 1.5x target | 2x+ target |
| Stage conversion rate | Above 50% | 30-50% | Below 30% |
| Multi-threading rate | 60%+ deals have 3+ contacts | 40-60% | Below 40% |
| Activity frequency | Action every 5-7 days | Every 8-14 days | 15+ day gaps |
| Forecast accuracy | Within 10% of actual | 10-25% deviation | 25%+ deviation |

## Emergency Playbooks for Stalled Deals

### Discovery Stall (No response after first call)

**Diagnosis**: Buyer is not engaged or pain is not urgent enough.

**72-Hour Rescue Plan**:
1. Day 1: Send meeting recap with insight (if not already sent)
2. Day 2: Send a follow-up with a relevant trigger event or case study
3. Day 3: Call directly with a specific question: "I wanted to follow up on [specific pain]. Has anything changed since our conversation?"
4. Day 5: Try a different stakeholder if champion is silent
5. Day 7: Honest email: "I want to be respectful of your time. Is this still something you're looking to address this quarter?"

### Evaluation Stall (Demo done, nothing happening)

**Diagnosis**: Usually one of: unresolved technical concern, missing economic buyer, competing priority, or competitor overtook you.

**1-Week Rescue Plan**:
1. Day 1: Call champion: "Where do things stand internally? Is there anything I can help with?"
2. Day 2: If concern identified, address it (reference call, technical deep-dive, competitive differentiation)
3. Day 3: If no champion response, contact the technical evaluator directly
4. Day 5: Send a time-bound proposal: "I'd like to put together a proposal based on what we discussed. Could we schedule 15 minutes to align on scope?"
5. Day 7: If silence continues, executive-to-executive outreach

### Proposal Stall (Proposal sent, no movement)

**Diagnosis**: Price shock, internal politics, competing priority, or the champion cannot sell it internally.

**5-Day Rescue Plan**:
1. Day 1-2: Call champion to check status
2. Day 3: If no response, email a "proposal highlights" summary (1-pager, not the full doc)
3. Day 4: Offer to present the proposal to the broader team: "Would it help if I walked the team through this?"
4. Day 5: Contact the economic buyer directly if the champion is stuck

### Negotiation Stall (Terms discussion going nowhere)

**Diagnosis**: Legal blocker, budget constraint, internal re-org, or buyer using you for leverage.

**1-Week Rescue Plan**:
1. Day 1: Identify the specific blocker (call champion)
2. Day 2-3: Address the blocker directly -- legal-to-legal call, revised pricing, procurement shortcut
3. Day 4: Offer a strategic concession tied to a deadline
4. Day 5: If buyer is using you for leverage, ask directly: "Are you still evaluating alternatives?"
5. Day 7: Escalate internally for manager review

### Closing Stall (Contract sent, not signed)

**Diagnosis**: Signatory unavailable, last-minute concern, internal change, or buyer's remorse.

**48-Hour Rescue Plan**:
1. Hour 4: Call to confirm receipt and ask if anything needs clarification
2. Hour 24: If not signed, call champion: "Is there anything holding this up?"
3. Hour 36: If champion is silent, contact the economic buyer or a different stakeholder
4. Hour 48: If no movement, preview the implementation plan to create forward momentum

## Time Limits and Escalation Rules

### Escalation Ladder

| Time Exceeded By | Escalation |
|---|---|
| 25% over target | Rep self-diagnoses: what is blocking this deal? |
| 50% over target | Manager review: is this deal still viable? |
| 100% over target (2x) | Leadership review: rescue, requalify, or close as lost |
| 150% over target | Strong recommendation to close as lost |

### The 2x Rule

A deal in any single stage for more than 2x the target duration has less than 15% chance of closing (InsightSquared, 100,000+ deals). At 2x, the deal must show a verifiable reason for delay or be closed as lost.

## Worked Example: A Model Deal Journey

### The Setup
**Company**: CloudMetrics Inc. (150 employees, Series B, B2B SaaS analytics)
**Deal Value**: $48,000/year
**Segment**: Mid-Market
**Timeline**: 45 days from first contact to close

### Day 1-5: Discovery

**Actions taken**:
1. **Day 1**: First call with VP of Sales, Jamie Park. Open-ended questions: "Walk me through how your team tracks pipeline health today." Jamie describes manual spreadsheet processes, 4 hours/week per rep wasted.
2. **Day 1**: During the call, identified CTO Alex Rivera as technical evaluator and CEO Maria Santos as economic buyer.
3. **Day 1 (same day)**: Sent meeting recap within 2 hours. Included insight: "Companies similar to CloudMetrics reduce pipeline reporting time by 85% -- from 4 hours to 35 minutes per rep per week."
4. **Day 3**: Jamie replied, confirmed the recap was accurate, and asked to schedule a demo.

**Exit criteria met**: Pain quantified ($4,800/month in lost rep time for 12 reps), 2 stakeholders identified, next step scheduled.

### Day 6-10: Qualification

**Actions taken**:
1. **Day 6**: Follow-up call with Jamie. Confirmed: budget exists (it is in Q1 tooling budget), Maria signs contracts over $25K, eval criteria are "time savings, integration with Salesforce, security compliance."
2. **Day 7**: Web search revealed CloudMetrics raised $18M Series B last month. Used this in conversation: "With the funding and hiring plans, this pain will scale -- 20 reps instead of 12 means 8 hours/week wasted instead of 4."
3. **Day 8**: Jamie confirmed she is the champion: "I have been pushing for this. Maria asked me to evaluate options."

**Exit criteria met**: BANT confirmed, champion identified (Jamie), compelling event (scaling pain with new hires post-funding).

### Day 11-25: Demo / Evaluation

**Actions taken**:
1. **Day 11**: Delivered demo customized to CloudMetrics' Salesforce setup, using their real pipeline stages. Jamie AND Alex attended.
2. **Day 14**: Alex raised concern about API rate limits and data residency (captured in transcript). Scheduled a technical deep-dive with SE for Day 16.
3. **Day 16**: Technical deep-dive resolved API concerns. Alex confirmed technical fit.
4. **Day 18**: Arranged reference call -- TechPulse (same industry, 200 employees, similar use case). Jamie and Alex attended. Post-call, Jamie said: "That was really helpful."
5. **Day 20**: Built ROI model: $57,600/year in recovered rep time, $48,000/year cost, 10-month payback, 20% ROI in year 1, 120% by year 3.
6. **Day 22**: Requested executive alignment with Maria. Jamie facilitated the introduction.
7. **Day 25**: 20-minute exec meeting with Maria and our VP. Maria asked about implementation timeline and security certification. Both addressed.

**Exit criteria met**: Demo done, 3 stakeholders engaged, technical fit confirmed, reference completed, ROI model built, EB engaged.

### Day 26-30: Proposal

**Actions taken**:
1. **Day 26**: Sent proposal with 3 tiers (Starter $36K, Professional $48K, Enterprise $72K). Professional tier matched their needs. Sent within 24 hours of Maria's meeting.
2. **Day 27**: Pre-wire call with Jamie: "What concerns do you think Maria will have?" Jamie: "She will ask about the implementation timeline and what happens if it does not work." Prepared: implementation plan preview and 90-day success guarantee.
3. **Day 28**: Jamie confirmed Professional tier selected. Maria wants to discuss payment terms.
4. **Day 29**: Identified procurement requirement: vendor security questionnaire (standard, 2-day turnaround).

**Exit criteria met**: Proposal reviewed, tier selected, procurement identified.

### Day 31-38: Negotiation

**Actions taken**:
1. **Day 31**: Maria's office requested Net-60 payment terms (standard was Net-30). Agreed -- low cost, high goodwill.
2. **Day 32**: Completed security questionnaire (submitted same day).
3. **Day 35**: Legal review flagged data processing addendum needed. Connected legal teams directly.
4. **Day 37**: DPA signed. All terms agreed.
5. **Day 38**: Contract finalized, ready for signature.

**Key moment**: On Day 34, Jamie went quiet for 2 days. Instead of waiting, called her: "Is everything OK on your end?" She revealed competing priority (board meeting prep). Timing issue, not deal issue. Patience, not panic.

### Day 39-41: Closing

**Actions taken**:
1. **Day 39**: Sent e-sign link (DocuSign, mobile-friendly, pre-filled). Confirmed Maria was available to sign.
2. **Day 39 (4 hours later)**: Not yet signed. Called Jamie: "Is Maria back from her board meeting?" Jamie: "She is reviewing it now."
3. **Day 40**: Signed. Payment terms confirmed. Sent implementation plan preview and scheduled kickoff for Day 45.
4. **Day 41**: Introduced customer success manager. Internal handoff meeting with full context from discovery through close, including all transcript summaries.

### Day 45: Post-Sale Kickoff

**Actions taken**: Kickoff meeting with Jamie, Alex, and CS. 90-day success plan shared: metric = reduce pipeline reporting time from 4 hours to 35 minutes per rep within 60 days. 30-day check-in scheduled.

### What Made This Deal Work

| Success Factor | Why It Mattered |
|---|---|
| Multi-threaded from Day 1 | 3 stakeholders meant no single point of failure |
| Pain quantified early | $4,800/month gave Maria budget justification |
| Trigger event leveraged | Series B funding created urgency and scale narrative |
| Customized demo | Used their actual Salesforce setup, not generic |
| Reference call timed perfectly | Post-demo, pre-proposal -- maximum impact |
| Proposal sent in 24 hours | No momentum lost after exec meeting |
| Pre-wired negotiation | Knew Maria's concerns before she raised them |
| DPA handled proactively | Legal did not become a bottleneck |
| Quick follow-up at closing | 4-hour follow-up on unsigned contract prevented drift |
| Honest communication | When Jamie went quiet, asked directly instead of assuming the worst |

### What Could Have Gone Wrong (and the Rescue Actions)

| Risk Point | What Would Happen | Rescue Action |
|---|---|---|
| Day 3: Jamie did not respond to recap | Deal stalls at discovery | Day 5: value-add email with industry report. Day 7: call. |
| Day 14: Alex raised technical blockers | Evaluation stalls | Same-day: schedule technical deep-dive with SE (did this) |
| Day 22: Maria unavailable for exec meeting | No EB engagement | Ask Jamie to forward the ROI model. Try a 10-min async video. |
| Day 27: Jamie did not share Maria's concerns | Surprise objections in negotiation | Prepare for top 3 objections based on company profile and similar deals |
| Day 34: Jamie went silent | Panic, over-follow-up | One call to check in. Wait 48 hours before escalating. (Did this correctly.) |
| Day 39: Maria did not sign same day | Contract drift | 4-hour follow-up call. Next day: preview implementation plan to create urgency. (Did this.) |
