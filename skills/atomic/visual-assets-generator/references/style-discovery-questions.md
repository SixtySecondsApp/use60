# Style Discovery Questions

Question bank for Phase 1 style discovery. Select 3-5 questions based on context.

---

## Universal Questions (ask 2-3 of these)

### 1. Visual Inspiration
> "What brands, websites, or apps have a visual style you admire? Even outside your industry — just anything that feels right."

**Why**: Fastest way to understand aesthetic preferences. Mentioning "Stripe" vs "Notion" vs "Nike" tells you everything.

### 2. Mood / Feeling
> "Which best describes the vibe you're going for?"
> - **Premium / Clean** — think Apple, Linear, Vercel
> - **Bold / Energetic** — think Nike, Spotify, Discord
> - **Warm / Approachable** — think Notion, Slack, Mailchimp
> - **Dark / Techy** — think GitHub, Raycast, Warp

**Why**: Maps directly to color temperature, contrast, and composition choices.

### 3. Color Direction
> "Any specific colors to use or avoid? Brand colors, favorite colors, or colors that feel wrong for this project."

**Why**: Prevents generating a moodboard in colors they'll immediately reject.

### 4. Context / Medium
> "Where will these visuals live? (Landing page, social media, email campaigns, pitch deck, app UI, print)"

**Why**: Determines aspect ratio, level of detail, and composition style.

### 5. References
> "Do you have any screenshots, competitor examples, or existing brand assets I should reference?"

**Why**: Concrete references eliminate ambiguity faster than descriptions.

---

## Marketing / Brand Questions (ask 1-2 if relevant)

### 6. Target Audience
> "Who's the audience? What should they feel when they see this?"

**Why**: A VC pitch deck looks different from a consumer app landing page.

### 7. Brand Positioning
> "Where do you want to sit on the spectrum: established/trustworthy vs innovative/cutting-edge?"

**Why**: Established = muted tones, clean layouts. Innovative = bold colors, dynamic compositions.

### 8. Existing Brand
> "Do you have existing brand guidelines, logos, or a style guide I should match?"

**Why**: If they have a brand, we need to align — not reinvent.

---

## Illustration Questions (ask 1-2 if relevant)

### 9. Illustration Style
> "Do you prefer a specific illustration style?"
> - **Flat / Vector** — simple shapes, bold colors (like Dropbox, Intercom)
> - **Isometric** — 3D-angled technical scenes (like Stripe, Miro)
> - **Hand-drawn** — organic, sketchy feel (like Basecamp, indie products)
> - **3D Rendered** — polished dimensional objects (like Apple, Figma)

**Why**: Illustration style is the single biggest factor in visual consistency.

### 10. Level of Detail
> "Simple and iconic, or rich and detailed?"

**Why**: Determines prompt complexity and composition density.

### 11. Human Representation
> "Should illustrations include people? If so, any diversity or style preferences?"

**Why**: People in illustrations require style decisions upfront — abstract vs realistic, diversity representation.

---

## Social Media Questions (ask 1-2 if relevant)

### 12. Platform Target
> "Which platforms? Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, TikTok — each has different visual norms."

**Why**: LinkedIn = professional/clean. Instagram = bold/eye-catching. Twitter = minimal/text-heavy.

### 13. Content Type
> "What type of content? (Product shots, quote graphics, data visualizations, behind-the-scenes, thought leadership)"

**Why**: Determines template structure and composition patterns.

### 14. Scroll-Stop Factor
> "How attention-grabbing should these be? Subtle and on-brand, or bold and scroll-stopping?"

**Why**: Directly affects contrast, color saturation, and composition dynamism.

---

## Skip Conditions

**Skip style discovery entirely when:**

- User provided **3+ style signals** in their initial request (e.g., "flat illustration, blue and purple, for a SaaS landing page")
- User says **"surprise me"** or **"just do it"** or **"dealer's choice"**
- User references a **specific brand/style** (e.g., "make it look like Linear")
- Continuing from a **previous session** with a locked style
- **Express route** — user wants a single quick image and described it well

**When skipping**, synthesize what they've given directly into a moodboard or first assets.
