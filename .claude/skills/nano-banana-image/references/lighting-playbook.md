# Lighting Playbook — The Master Key to Image Quality

Lighting is the single most impactful element in any image prompt. The same subject with different lighting produces completely different emotional responses. Master this playbook and every image improves.

---

## The 12 Lighting Moods

### 1. Professional / Clean

**When to use:** Corporate hero images, team photos, product shots, landing pages for B2B SaaS.

```
Soft, even studio lighting from slightly above and to the left.
Subtle fill light from the right reducing shadows to 20%.
Clean white or light gray background.
No dramatic shadows. Flattering, even illumination.
```

**Key words:** "soft diffused lighting", "even illumination", "subtle shadows", "studio quality"

### 2. Bold / Editorial

**When to use:** Thought leadership content, brand campaigns, feature announcements.

```
Strong directional key light from the left at 45 degrees.
Deep shadows on the right side (50-70% shadow density).
High contrast. Dramatic chiaroscuro effect.
Background falls to near-black on the shadow side.
```

**Key words:** "dramatic side lighting", "high contrast", "editorial style", "strong shadows"

### 3. Warm / Approachable

**When to use:** Community pages, about us, onboarding, customer success stories.

```
Warm golden light from behind the subject (back-rim lighting).
Soft lens flare from the light source.
Fill light from the front keeps subject visible.
Warm color temperature throughout (3200K-4000K).
```

**Key words:** "golden hour lighting", "warm backlight", "soft lens flare", "inviting atmosphere"

### 4. Tech / Futuristic

**When to use:** AI features, developer tools, innovation pages, tech product launches.

```
Cool blue rim lighting from behind, separating subject from dark background.
Neon accent glow from screens or UI elements (cyan, magenta).
Dark environment with controlled light bloom effects.
Light reflects off surfaces creating tech-like ambiance.
```

**Key words:** "cool blue rim lighting", "neon accent glow", "dark environment", "light bloom"

### 5. Elegant / Luxury

**When to use:** Premium tiers, enterprise features, high-end brand positioning.

```
Soft gradient lighting from directly above, creating a halo effect.
Subtle specular highlights on metallic or glass surfaces.
Rich, deep tonal range — no blown highlights, no crushed blacks.
Minimal shadows, maximum richness.
```

**Key words:** "soft gradient lighting", "specular highlights", "rich tonal depth", "luxury lighting"

### 6. Energetic / Startup

**When to use:** Launch announcements, growth content, social media, early-stage brand.

```
Bright, even daylight filling the entire scene.
Vibrant colors fully saturated — nothing muted.
Clean white backdrop or bright environment.
No moody shadows. Everything optimistic and visible.
```

**Key words:** "bright daylight", "vibrant colors", "clean white backdrop", "high-key lighting"

### 7. Moody / Atmospheric

**When to use:** Security features, privacy, data protection, thought-provoking content.

```
Low-key lighting — most of the image is dark.
Single focused light beam cutting through fog or haze.
Volumetric fog catching and scattering the light.
Dramatic chiaroscuro. Subject partially in shadow.
```

**Key words:** "low-key lighting", "volumetric fog", "single light beam", "dramatic chiaroscuro"

### 8. Natural / Authentic

**When to use:** Blog posts, case studies, behind-the-scenes, culture content.

```
Natural window light — soft, directional, unmodified.
Gentle shadows that follow the sun's natural direction.
Warm but not exaggerated color temperature.
No artificial lighting feel. Candid, documentary quality.
```

**Key words:** "natural window light", "unposed", "documentary lighting", "candid"

### 9. Neon Night

**When to use:** Entertainment, nightlife, creative industries, bold campaigns.

```
Multiple neon light sources in contrasting colors (cyan + magenta, or purple + orange).
Wet surfaces reflecting neon colors (rain-slicked streets, glass, metal).
Deep shadows between light pools.
Volumetric color in the air from each neon source.
```

**Key words:** "neon sign lighting", "wet surface reflections", "cyan and magenta hues", "night scene"

### 10. Sunset Gradient

**When to use:** Year-end reviews, journey content, milestone celebrations, transition pages.

```
Gradient sky lighting shifting from warm gold at horizon to deep blue/purple above.
Long shadows stretching horizontally.
Warm light on subject faces, cool sky reflected on top surfaces.
Magic hour glow — skin tones look perfect.
```

**Key words:** "sunset gradient", "golden to blue transition", "magic hour", "long horizontal shadows"

### 11. Overhead / Flat Lay

**When to use:** Product collections, workspace shots, tool showcases.

```
Perfectly even overhead lighting, no directional shadows.
Soft box directly above the flat lay arrangement.
Minimal shadows — just enough to give depth (5-10%).
Colors appear exactly as intended, no warm/cool bias.
```

**Key words:** "overhead soft box", "flat lay lighting", "even illumination", "no directional shadows"

### 12. Silhouette / Backlit

**When to use:** Inspirational content, hero images, aspirational brand messaging.

```
Strong backlight completely behind the subject.
Subject appears as a dark silhouette against bright background.
Rim light catches edges creating a glowing outline.
Background can be solid color gradient or dramatic sky.
```

**Key words:** "silhouette", "strong backlight", "rim light outline", "dark subject against bright background"

## Advanced Technique: Color Temperature Mixing

The pro technique that separates amateur from professional lighting. Mix warm and cool light sources.

```
BASIC (single temperature):
"Warm lighting throughout"

PRO (mixed temperatures):
"Key light: warm tungsten (3200K) desk lamp from the left, creating golden highlights.
Fill: cool daylight (6500K) from a window on the right, blue-tinted shadows.
Accent: magenta neon reflected in a glass surface behind the subject.
The color temperature contrast creates visual depth and interest."
```

### Common Pro Combinations

| Warm Source | Cool Source | Effect |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Desk lamp | Monitor glow | Late-night work atmosphere |
| Sunset light | Shade shadow | Natural outdoor depth |
| Practical lights | Window daylight | Authentic interior |
| Golden spotlight | Blue ambient | Stage/presentation drama |
| Candle/fire | Moonlight | Intimate warmth vs. cool distance |

## Lighting Direction Quick Reference

```
FROM ABOVE: Even, neutral, professional, "studio"
FROM LEFT: Standard key light, classic portrait, most natural
FROM RIGHT: Unconventional, can feel dramatic or uneasy
FROM BEHIND: Silhouette, rim glow, dramatic, aspirational
FROM BELOW: Unnatural, dramatic (rarely used for marketing)
FROM FRONT: Flat, even, beauty-style, "ring light"
45° UPPER LEFT: The "golden angle" — universally flattering
```

## Atmosphere Modifiers

Add these to any lighting setup for extra depth:

| Modifier | Description | Prompt Text |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Fog** | Softens all light, creates depth | "Light fog diffusing the light sources into soft halos" |
| **Rain** | Adds reflections everywhere | "Light rain adding subtle reflections on every surface" |
| **Dust particles** | Visible in light beams | "Dust particles catching the light beam, visible in the air" |
| **Lens flare** | Cinematic, aspirational | "Subtle lens flare from the primary light source" |
| **Bokeh** | Background blur circles | "City lights creating colorful bokeh circles in background" |
| **God rays** | Light beams through gaps | "Volumetric light beams streaming through window blinds" |
| **Smoke/haze** | Atmospheric density | "Thin atmospheric haze adding depth to the scene" |
