# Chase Templates by Silence Duration

This reference document provides ready-to-adapt templates organized by how long the prospect has been silent. Each template includes subject line, opening, value-add, CTA, and timing guidance. Templates are provided in three tones (warm, direct, humorous) and across four channels (email, LinkedIn, voicemail, text).

**How to use this document:** Select the duration tier that matches the silence analysis from Layer 4. Choose the tone based on relationship depth and deal context. Adapt the template using specific data from Layers 1-3 -- never send a template without personalizing it with real context.

---

## Tier 1: Light Touch (5-7 Days Silent)

**Psychology:** The prospect is likely busy, not disengaged. This is normal sales cadence. The goal is to stay visible without applying pressure. Add value, not urgency.

### Email Templates

#### Warm Tone (Default)

**Subject:** Quick thought on [topic from last call]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

After our conversation about [specific topic], I came across [article/data point/case study] that connects directly to what you mentioned about [their stated challenge]. Thought it might be useful as you think through [their initiative].

[1-2 sentence summary of the resource and why it matters to them specifically.]

No rush at all -- would a quick 10-minute call this week be useful to dig into this?

Best,
[Sender]

**Timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-9am in their timezone. Early morning emails get opened before inbox overwhelm.

#### Direct Tone

**Subject:** [Their company] + [your topic] -- one idea

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

Following up on our [day of week] conversation. I had one additional thought about [specific point they raised]:

[Concrete insight, data point, or recommendation -- 2 sentences max.]

Worth a quick call to explore this, or has the focus shifted elsewhere?

[Sender]

#### Humorous Tone (Use Only with Established Rapport)

**Subject:** Not another follow-up email

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I know your inbox is a warzone, so I will be brief. Remember when we talked about [specific thing]? I found something you will actually want to see: [resource/insight].

If 10 minutes on [day] works, I am buying the virtual coffee. If not, no hard feelings -- I will just cry quietly at my desk.

[Sender]

### LinkedIn Message (40-60 Words)

Hi [First Name] -- enjoyed our chat about [topic] last [day]. Came across [insight/article] that ties into what you mentioned about [challenge]. Thought you would find it useful. Happy to share more context if helpful.

### Voicemail Script (30 Seconds)

"Hi [First Name], it is [Your Name] from [Company]. Quick call -- after our conversation about [topic], I came across something I think you will find useful for [their challenge]. I will drop you a note with the details, but wanted to flag it. Talk soon."

### Text Message (Only If Prior Text Relationship Exists)

"Hey [First Name] -- found something relevant to our [topic] chat. Sending it over by email. Let me know if you want to jump on a quick call about it."

## Tier 2: Pattern Break (8-14 Days Silent)

**Psychology:** Two weeks of silence means something shifted -- priorities, internal dynamics, or interest. The "same angle, louder" approach fails here. You need a new angle, fresh information, or a different value proposition to break the pattern.

### Email Templates

#### Warm Tone

**Subject:** [Something new] for [their company]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

Since we last spoke, I noticed [trigger event: their company news, industry development, or competitor move]. It made me think about our conversation on [specific topic] from a different angle.

[2 sentences connecting the trigger event to their stated problem or goal.]

I know things shift fast -- if this is still on your radar, I would love to share how [specific value proposition]. A quick 15-minute call this week?

Best,
[Sender]

**Timing:** Wednesday or Thursday, 4-5pm. Late-week, end-of-day emails get more thoughtful reads as people wind down.

#### Direct Tone

**Subject:** [First Name] -- different angle on [topic]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I have been thinking about what you mentioned regarding [their specific challenge]. Rather than rehashing our last conversation, I wanted to share a different perspective:

[New data point, different case study, or alternative approach -- 2-3 sentences.]

Is this still a priority for [their company] this quarter? Happy to adjust my approach based on where things stand.

[Sender]

#### Humorous Tone

**Subject:** Plot twist on [topic]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

Remember [specific thing from last conversation]? Well, plot twist -- [new development/insight that changes the picture].

I promise this is not just a creative way to follow up (okay, maybe partly). But genuinely, [1 sentence on why this matters to them].

Got 15 minutes this week? I will keep it tighter than a TED talk.

[Sender]

### LinkedIn Message

Hi [First Name] -- noticed [company news or trigger event]. It connects to what we discussed about [topic]. I have a fresh take on this that might be worth 15 minutes. Open to a quick chat this week?

### Voicemail Script

"Hi [First Name], [Your Name] from [Company]. I know it has been a couple weeks since we connected. I am not calling to rehash -- I actually have a new angle on [topic] based on [trigger event or new insight]. Going to send you a quick email with the details. Would love 15 minutes if it is relevant."

### Text Message

"Hey [First Name] -- saw [trigger event]. Made me think of our conversation. Sent you an email with a fresh angle. Worth a look."

## Tier 3: Direct Check-In (15-21 Days Silent)

**Psychology:** Three weeks of silence is a signal, not an accident. The prospect may have deprioritized, hit internal resistance, or chosen a competitor. Continued value-add emails feel tone-deaf at this point. Acknowledge the gap directly and ask the honest question.

### Email Templates

#### Warm Tone

**Subject:** Checking in -- [First Name]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

It has been a few weeks since we connected about [topic], and I wanted to check in directly. I know priorities can shift quickly, especially with [reference to their known situation -- quarter end, product launch, org changes].

If [their initiative] is still on the table, I am here to help. If things have changed direction, I completely understand -- just let me know so I can adjust on my end.

Either way, I hope [something genuinely positive about their company or situation].

Best,
[Sender]

**Timing:** Monday or Tuesday, 9-10am. Start-of-week sends for important conversations.

#### Direct Tone

**Subject:** [First Name] -- quick honest question

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I want to respect your time, so I will be direct: when we last spoke about [specific topic], you mentioned [their stated timeline or commitment]. I have not heard back since, and I want to make sure I am not missing something.

Has the priority shifted, or is the timing just not right? Either answer is completely fine -- I would rather know than keep following up into silence.

[Sender]

#### Humorous Tone

**Subject:** Am I in your spam folder?

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

At this point, I have three theories: (1) my emails are going to spam, (2) you have been abducted by aliens, or (3) things just got busy. I am betting on option 3.

Seriously though -- I know [topic] was important to you when we last talked. If it still is, I am here. If not, just say the word and I will stop haunting your inbox.

[Sender]

### LinkedIn Message

Hi [First Name] -- it has been a few weeks since our conversation about [topic]. I wanted to check in directly: is this still a priority, or have things shifted? Either way, no pressure -- just want to make sure I am being helpful, not noisy.

### Voicemail Script

"Hi [First Name], [Your Name] from [Company]. I realize it has been about three weeks since we last connected on [topic]. I am not trying to be persistent for the sake of it -- I genuinely want to know if this is still on your radar or if priorities have shifted. A quick call or even a one-line email either way would be great. I will follow up with a short note. Thanks."

## Tier 4: The Breakup (22-30 Days Silent)

**Psychology:** A month of silence after active engagement is a strong signal. This is the "permission to close" email -- one of the highest-response-rate formats in sales. It works because it removes pressure and gives the prospect control. People respond when they feel they are being let go, not pursued.

### Email Templates

#### Warm Tone (The Classic Breakup)

**Subject:** Should I close the loop?

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I have reached out a few times since our conversation about [topic] and have not heard back. I do not want to be a nuisance, so I wanted to check one last time:

Should I keep [their initiative/project] on my radar, or would you prefer I close this out for now?

No hard feelings either way. If things change down the road, my door is always open.

All the best,
[Sender]

**Timing:** Tuesday, 8am. The breakup email deserves prime inbox real estate.

**Why this works:** Research shows breakup emails get 2-3x the response rate of standard follow-ups. The permission-to-close frame triggers loss aversion -- people do not want to lose options, even options they are not actively pursuing.

#### Direct Tone

**Subject:** Closing the loop on [topic]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I want to be respectful of your time. I am going to assume [their initiative] has either been deprioritized or you have gone in a different direction -- both completely understandable.

I am going to close this out on my end unless I hear otherwise. If circumstances change in the future, you know where to find me.

Wishing you and the [their company] team all the best.

[Sender]

#### Humorous Tone

**Subject:** Permission to stop emailing you

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I think we both know I have been talking to myself for the last few weeks. Rather than become that person who sends "just checking in" emails until the heat death of the universe, I am going to bow out gracefully.

If [topic] comes back around, you have my number. In the meantime, I will be over here, working on my follow-up email addiction.

All the best,
[Sender]

### LinkedIn Message

Hi [First Name] -- I have followed up a few times on [topic] and want to be respectful. I am going to close this out unless you think there is still a conversation to be had. No pressure at all -- wishing you well.

### Voicemail Script (The Final Call)

"Hi [First Name], [Your Name]. This is going to be a short one. I have followed up a few times on [topic] and I do not want to overstay my welcome. I am going to close this out on my end. If things change, my door is open. I wish you all the best. Take care."

## Tier 5: Trigger-Event Re-Activation (30+ Days Silent)

**Psychology:** After 30 days, the original conversation is cold. Do NOT send another follow-up -- it reads as desperate. The only justifiable reason to re-engage after this long is a genuine trigger event: company news, leadership change, funding round, industry shift, or a relevant development that creates a new reason to talk. Without a trigger event, move to graceful exit.

### Email Templates (Trigger-Event Based Only)

#### Company News Trigger

**Subject:** Congrats on [their news] -- quick thought

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

Saw the news about [specific trigger event -- funding round, acquisition, product launch, expansion]. Congratulations to you and the team.

It made me think back to our conversation about [topic] -- specifically [how the trigger event connects to their stated challenge or goal]. [1 sentence on why this changes the picture.]

If the timing is better now, I would love to reconnect. If not, genuinely happy to see [their company] doing well.

Best,
[Sender]

#### Role Change Trigger

**Subject:** Congrats on the new role, [First Name]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

Noticed you moved to [new role/company] -- congratulations. That is a great move.

When we talked previously about [topic], you were dealing with [their challenge]. Curious if that is something you are thinking about in your new role as well? If so, I have some updated thinking that might be useful.

Either way, best of luck in the new position.

[Sender]

#### Industry Event Trigger

**Subject:** [Industry development] -- thought of our conversation

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

[Industry development -- regulation change, competitor acquisition, market shift] caught my attention this week, and it reminded me of our conversation about [their specific situation].

[1-2 sentences connecting the development to their context.]

If this is relevant to what [their company] is working on, happy to chat. If not, no worries -- just wanted to flag it.

Best,
[Sender]

### Graceful Exit (No Trigger Event Found)

If no trigger event exists after 30+ days, do not send another chase. Instead, recommend:

1. **Add to re-engagement watch list:** Monitor for trigger events via web search, LinkedIn, and company news
2. **Set a 90-day reminder:** Check back in 90 days for natural re-engagement opportunities
3. **Document the outcome:** Update CRM with "chase exhausted -- monitoring for trigger events"

## Annotated Good vs Bad Examples

### 5-7 Day Example

**Bad:**
> Subject: Following up
> Hi Sarah, just wanted to follow up on our conversation last week. Have you had a chance to think about our proposal? Let me know if you have any questions. Looking forward to hearing from you.

**Why it fails:** Generic subject. "Just following up" is about the seller. No value-add. "Let me know" is a weak CTA. Reads like a template.

**Good:**
> Subject: That pipeline problem you mentioned
> Hi Sarah, after our chat about the pipeline visibility gap, I ran across Gartner's latest RevOps benchmark -- turns out 67% of mid-market teams face the exact bottleneck you described. Thought it might be useful context as you scope the project. Happy to walk through how others solved it if you have 10 minutes this week.

**Why it works:** Specific reference to their problem. Adds genuine value (data point). CTA is small and specific.

### 15-21 Day Example

**Bad:**
> Subject: Checking in again
> Hi Sarah, I have reached out a few times and have not heard back. I know you are busy but I really think we could help you with your pipeline issues. Can we schedule a call this week?

**Why it fails:** "Checking in again" signals persistence without value. "I really think" is seller-centric. Guilt-trips about previous outreach.

**Good:**
> Subject: Sarah -- honest question
> Hi Sarah, it has been a few weeks since our conversation about pipeline visibility. I know Q1 planning can consume everything. I wanted to ask directly: is this still on your radar for this quarter, or has the priority shifted? Either answer is fine -- I would just rather know than keep guessing. Hope Q1 is off to a strong start.

**Why it works:** Acknowledges the gap without guilt. Asks the honest question. Gives permission to say no. Specific seasonal context.

### 22-30 Day Example (Breakup)

**Bad:**
> Subject: Last attempt
> Hi Sarah, this is my final email. I have tried reaching you multiple times about our pipeline solution. If I do not hear back, I will assume you are not interested. Please let me know either way.

**Why it fails:** "Last attempt" is aggressive. "Tried reaching you multiple times" guilt-trips. Ultimatum framing. Transactional, not human.

**Good:**
> Subject: Should I close the loop?
> Hi Sarah, I have followed up a few times since our pipeline visibility conversation and have not connected. I do not want to be a nuisance. Should I keep this on my radar, or would you prefer I close it out? No hard feelings either way -- if things change down the road, I am always happy to reconnect. Best to you and the team.

**Why it works:** Gives the prospect control. "Close it out" is non-threatening. "No hard feelings" is genuine. Leaves the door open without clinging.
