# Web Brief: 60 Landing Page V7

## Executive Summary
Rebuild use60.com to spacebot.sh quality — deeper content, architecture explanation, product showcase, richer feature depth. 60 is an AI sales command center with 127+ skills that automates everything before and after the sales call. The current V6 has great visual design but shallow content. V7 keeps the proven URL demo hero and adds the content depth, technical credibility, and section variety that makes spacebot.sh compelling.

## Audience & Awareness

**Primary persona**: Solo founder or small sales team lead (1-5 reps). Revenue under $5M. Doing founder-led sales. Lives in their calendar. Neglects follow-ups, pipeline hygiene, and meeting prep because they're too busy with "founder stuff."

**Secondary persona**: Sales manager at growing company (5-50 reps). Wants pipeline visibility without chasing reps for updates. Needs team-level coaching and deal intelligence.

**Awareness level**: Problem-aware to Solution-aware. They KNOW follow-ups are dropping and pipeline is stale. They may have tried point tools (CRM, notetaker, email tool) but nothing connects.

**Traffic sources**:
- Direct (word of mouth, early access referrals)
- Google organic ("AI sales tool", "automated follow-ups", "meeting prep AI")
- LLM referrals (ChatGPT/Claude recommendations — 15.9% conversion rate)
- LinkedIn (founder content, thought leadership)

## Conversion Strategy

**Hero pattern**: Product-as-hero. URL input → instant demo. UNCHANGED from V6 — proven mechanism.

**CTA strategy**: Single primary (URL input). Secondary "Try it free" buttons at mid-page (demo gate) and bottom (final CTA). No demo booking, no contact sales. Pure PLG.

**Social proof**: Metric-led proof bar below hero. Testimonials near bottom. Integration logos build trust. Tech credibility section builds engineering confidence.

**Demo strategy**: Interactive — the URL demo IS the product. No video, no screenshots, no tour. But NOW supplemented by product showcase panels showing real 60 outputs (follow-up emails, meeting preps, pipeline alerts) so visitors who don't try the demo still see the product working.

**New conversion path**: Visitor who scrolls past hero demo → sees product showcase panels (still impressed) → reads architecture (understands the "how") → sees feature depth (convinced of breadth) → integration grid (knows it works with their stack) → workflow case study (envisions their day) → converts.

## SEO Architecture

**Primary page**: Homepage (use60.com)
**LLM optimization**: Structure content with clear headings and concise answers. "What is 60?" answerable from hero + architecture. "How does 60 work?" answerable from architecture + how-it-works. "What integrations does 60 have?" answerable from integration grid.

## Product-Type Intelligence

**Style direction**: V6's "Electric Depth" didn't work well. Need a new direction in DESIGN phase.

**Recommendations for new style**:
- Still dark mode (premium positioning, industry standard for AI/sales tools)
- Less "electric" — tone down the violet glow, reduce atmosphere effects
- More structured/editorial — let the content breathe (spacebot.sh uses generous spacing)
- Clean, high-contrast — white text on near-black, minimal color distractions
- Technical confidence — feels like infrastructure, not marketing fluff
- Consider: closer to Linear's restraint, Vercel's clarity, or Resend's editorial feel

**Typography direction**: Still needs distinctive display font. Body font should prioritize readability at long-form content lengths (the deep-dives have more text than V6's sections).

**UX patterns**:
- Single-page scroll with section anchors
- Sticky navbar with CTA
- Product showcase: tabbed panels or auto-cycling
- Feature grid: expandable cards or hover-reveal sub-items
- Workflow case study: timeline/scroll-linked animation
- Mobile: full-width sections, stacked cards, collapsible deep-dives

## Design Direction Notes

Electric Depth was too heavy on atmosphere effects and not structured enough for content-rich sections. V7 needs a style that:

1. **Handles information density** — generous spacing, clear hierarchy, scannable layouts
2. **Supports long-form content** — readable body text, clear section transitions
3. **Feels premium without being flashy** — confidence through restraint, not glow effects
4. **Works for product showcase panels** — needs clean card/panel components
5. **Supports diagrams and data** — architecture diagrams, integration grids, feature matrices need clear visual treatment

Style references to explore:
- Linear.app — clean dark, great spacing, lets content lead
- Resend.com — editorial dark, strong typography, minimal decoration
- Vercel.com — high-contrast, grid-based, technical confidence
- Raycast.com — warm dark, approachable, great feature showcases
